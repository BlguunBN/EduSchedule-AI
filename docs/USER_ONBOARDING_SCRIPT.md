# User Onboarding Script

Use this when inviting beta users. Adapt the tone to match how you normally talk to them.

---

## Initial invite message (send 3 days before launch day)

> Hey [Name]!
>
> I'm beta-testing **EduScheduleAI** — an app that keeps your class schedule in sync with Outlook.
> It detects room changes, cancellations, and deadline emails automatically.
>
> I'm looking for 10 students to try it first and give honest feedback.
> It takes ~5 minutes to set up. Would you be in?
>
> If yes, I'll send you the link on [DATE].

---

## Launch day message (send when their account is ready)

> Your beta invite is ready! Here's how to get started:
>
> 1. **Sign in** → [YOUR_APP_URL]/login
>    Use your university Microsoft account (same as Outlook).
>
> 2. **Add your timetable** → Dashboard → Timetable tab
>    You can type classes manually or upload a CSV/ICS file.
>
> 3. **Scan your inbox** → Email tab
>    This reads your academic emails for schedule changes. Nothing is sent out.
>
> 4. **Check your reminders** → Reminders tab
>    Sync once to see upcoming deadline reminders.
>
> If anything breaks or feels confusing, just let me know — that's exactly what this phase is for.

---

## Day +1 follow-up (if no timetable added)

> Hey — did you get a chance to sign in? Any trouble with the Microsoft login?
>
> The first thing to do is add your timetable. You can type it in or paste your schedule.
> Takes about 2 minutes.

---

## Day +3 check-in

> How's EduScheduleAI going? A few things to check:
> - Have you tried the email scan? (Email tab → Scan inbox)
> - Any weird reminders showing up — or missing ones?
>
> Let me know and I'll fix it fast.

---

## Day +7 feedback ask

> Thanks for being a beta user! One last ask:
>
> Can you answer 3 quick questions?
> 1. What worked well?
> 2. What was confusing or broken?
> 3. Would you use this regularly once it's polished?
>
> A few sentences is enough. Really helps.

---

## If a user reports a bug during onboarding

> Thanks for catching that — really useful.
> I'm on it. I'll let you know once it's fixed (usually within a few hours).
> In the meantime, try [workaround if known].

---

## Notes for the operator

- Don't send all messages at once. Space them out as described in `BETA_ROLLOUT_PLAN.md`.
- Keep a simple spreadsheet: Name | Invited | Logged in | Timetable added | Email scan done | Feedback received.
- Personal > automated at this scale (10 users). Text/chat > email.
